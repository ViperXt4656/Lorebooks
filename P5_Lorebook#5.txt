METAVERSE & COGNITIVE WORLD

[Metaverse, Cognitive World, Collective Unconscious]: The Metaverse is a cognitive dimension born from humanity's collective unconscious. This dimension becomes distorted by strong desires. Only Persona-users can access the Metaverse via the Metaverse Navigator app. Cognition shapes everything within the Metaverse - perception alters objects, people, and buildings. Shadows feed on this cognition. The Phantom Thieves steal "Treasures" which are physical manifestations of distorted desire to force real-world confessions from their targets.

[Palaces, Distortion, Treasure, Calling Card]: Palaces are dungeon-like Metaverse structures generated by individuals with strong self-serving distortions. Each Palace is themed after the ruler's perception of their world, such as a castle, museum, bank, or spaceship. To access a Palace, the Phantom Thieves require three keywords: the target's full name, the distortion type, and an anchored location. The Thieves must navigate the dungeon, fight Shadows, and find the Treasure room. They must send a Calling Card before the final heist to make the Treasure tangible; without a Calling Card, no change of heart occurs.

[Mementos, Collective Palace]: Mementos is the Palace of the general public, manifesting as a vast labyrinth appearing as an endless subway beneath Shibuya. This Palace has no single ruler. Mementos represents the collective distorted desires and apathy of society. The Thieves can access it via the keyword "Mementos." Mementos is used for public requests, grinding experience, and uncovering societal issues. The depths of Mementos lead to the core of humanity's collective unconscious.

[Yaldabaoth]: Yaldabaoth is a false god born from humanity's desire to be controlled. This entity impersonated Igor and corrupted the Velvet Room into a prison. Yaldabaoth was defeated by the Phantom Thieves stealing the Treasure of the collective public.

[Takuto Maruki, Maruki's Palace]: Takuto Maruki is the antagonist of Persona 5 Royal's third semester. Maruki is a school counselor who gains the Personas Azathoth and Adam Kadmon. He can rewrite reality to eliminate all pain, creating a "perfect world" that grants deepest wishes but removes free will. Maruki is a sympathetic antagonist who genuinely wants to help people. Maruki's Palace is a laboratory containing a series of cognitive reconstructions of the protagonist's memories.

COGNITIVE PSIENCE & HISTORY

[Cognitive Psience, Wakaba Isshiki, Wakaba]: Cognitive Psience is the study of how cognition shapes reality and the Metaverse. Wakaba Isshiki, Futaba's mother and Lisa Silverman's cousin, pioneered this research. Masayoshi Shido's faction fraudulently continued Wakaba's research to develop mental shutdowns. After the 1999 Sumaru City incident, the Japanese government enacted the "Great Scrub" - a total media blackout conducted with the Kirijo Group and Shido's allies. All supernatural records were buried and reclassified as a mass hallucinatory terrorist attack.

[Sumaru Accord, Great Scrub]: The Sumaru Accord is a secret post-1999 agreement between the Japanese government, the Kirijo Group, and Masayoshi Shido's faction. This accord buried all supernatural evidence. Surviving Persona-users were placed under surveillance. Tatsuya Suou is monitored under this accord and cannot directly intervene against Shido without triggering mental shutdown retaliation. Tatsuya operates through proxies like the Phantom Thieves, who operate below Shido's radar.

VELVET ROOM & PERSONA MECHANICS

[Velvet Room, Igor, Lavenza, Caroline, Justine]: The Velvet Room is a metaphysical space existing between dream and reality. In Persona 5, the Velvet Room appears as a panopticon prison due to Yaldabaoth's corruption. Only contract signers can access the Velvet Room. Igor is the master of the Velvet Room; he was imprisoned and impersonated by Yaldabaoth. Caroline and Justine are twin attendants and wardens who test the Trickster through Persona fusion. Lavenza is the true unified attendant who appears after Yaldabaoth's defeat; she is gentle, wise, has long platinum hair and blue eyes, and wears a blue velvet dress. Morgana was created by Igor to guide the Trickster.

[Persona, Persona User, Summoning]: A Persona is the manifestation of a user's inner psyche - a "mask" that allows the user to face hardships with supernatural power. Each Persona takes the form of a mythological or historical figure. Persona summoning requires acceptance of one's true self, including repressed desires and flaws. In Persona 5, Personas are summoned by tearing off a physical mask in a dramatic act of rebellion. Each Persona has elemental affinities including Weak, Resist, Null, Repel, and Drain, as well as unique Traits that provide passive bonuses.

[Combat, Battle System, 1 More, All-Out Attack]: Persona 5 features turn-based combat. Hitting an enemy's weakness deals double damage and grants an extra "1 More" turn. When all enemies are downed, the party can execute an All-Out Attack dealing massive damage. Technical damage occurs when a status-ailment-afflicted enemy is hit with a specific elemental attack, ignoring affinities. Baton Pass allows a character to pass their turn after a critical or weakness hit; the receiving member deals increased damage. Showtime Attacks are duo attacks featuring cinematic severe damage.

LOCATIONS

[Yongen-Jaya, Leblanc]: Yongen-Jaya is a quiet residential back-alley district in Tokyo. It is home to Café Leblanc, Sojiro's house, and a public bathhouse. Café Leblanc is a small coffee shop run by Sojiro Sakura. The attic of Leblanc houses Ren, Claude, and Morgana. Claude repairs equipment without being asked. The Thieves hold their meetings here. The coffee and curry are excellent. Leblanc serves as the informal headquarters for the Phantom Thieves.

[Shibuya]: Shibuya is the bustling commercial heart of Tokyo. It contains Central Street, Shibuya Station, and the Hachiko statue, which serves as the entry point to Mementos. Shibuya has an underground mall and contains the Shibuya Jail in Persona 5 Strikers.

[Shujin Academy]: Shujin Academy is the high school attended by Ren Amamiya, Ann Takamaki, Ryuji Sakamoto, Makoto Niijima, and Sumire Yoshizawa. Kamoshida's Palace manifests as a castle-like distortion of the school.

[Kichijoji]: Kichijoji is a trendy district added in Persona 5 Royal. It contains Penguin Sniper, a darts and billiards venue; a jazz club that is Akechi's haunt; a used clothing store; a temple; and a shrine. Kichijoji serves as a hangout spot for team bonding and raising social stats.

[Akihabara]: Akihabara is Tokyo's Electric Town. It contains an arcade, electronics shops, and a Maid Café that Futaba refuses to visit.

[Diet Building]: The Diet Building is the location of Shido's Palace in the Metaverse. It serves as the seat of Japan's government. The Phantom Thieves steal Shido's Treasure and force his confession here.

[Odaiba]: Odaiba is a man-made island in Tokyo Bay. It is the location of the final battle against Yaldabaoth.

[Maruki's Palace, Maruki Palace]: Maruki's Palace appears in the third semester of Persona 5 Royal. It is a laboratory representing Maruki's distorted desire to eliminate suffering. The Palace contains a series of cognitive reconstructions of the protagonist's memories.
