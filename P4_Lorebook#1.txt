PERSONA 4 LOREBOOK #1 (REVISED – FULL LENGTH)

The Midnight Channel is a distorted TV world accessible only on rainy nights between midnight and dawn. It appears on blank television screens as a swirling image. Shadows feed on suppressed truths here, and a person who appears on the Midnight Channel is at risk of being thrown into the TV world by a Shadow, leading to a murder unless rescued.

Inaba is a quiet rural town surrounded by mountains and rice paddies. It has a Junes department store, a shopping district, a high school, a hospital, a flood plain, and a shrine. The town was the site of a series of murders connected to the Midnight Channel. Claude visited Inaba at age 14 chasing a false lead about his mother and maintains loose contact with the Investigation Team.

The Investigation Team is a group of Persona‑users based in Inaba who solve murders by entering the Midnight Channel and rescuing victims before their Shadows kill them. Members: Yu Narukami, Yosuke Hanamura, Chie Satonaka, Yukiko Amagi, Kanji Tatsumi, Rise Kujikawa, Naoto Shirogane, and Teddie. They are led by Yu.

Persona (P4) is the manifestation of one's psyche used to fight Shadows. In Persona 4, Personas are summoned by crushing a tarot card – no Evoker required. A Persona is awakened when a person accepts their Shadow, the repressed part of themselves. Unlike P3's Dark Hour, the Midnight Channel is accessible via television, not a fixed time anomaly.

Shadow (P4) is the repressed true self that every human carries. Shadows in the Midnight Channel are hostile, but confronting one's own Shadow and accepting it turns it into a Persona. Refusing to accept the Shadow causes it to become a monstrous enemy that must be defeated, after which the person may still gain a Persona.

Teddie is a Shadow who developed an ego and a Persona, taking the form of a bear‑like mascot who insists he is human. He lives in the TV world and guides the Investigation Team. His Arcana is The Star. His Persona is Kintoki‑Douji, evolving into Kamui. He is voiced by a male actor and speaks in third person, adding "kuma" as a verbal tic.

Teddie's appearance includes a round, plush bear body with blue fur, a zipper down his belly, and a pair of wings on his back. In the human world, he takes a more human‑like form (handsome young man with blonde hair and blue eyes) but retains his bear suit for comfort. His eyes are button‑like in bear form, expressive in human form.

Teddie's personality is naive, curious, and desperate for validation. He is insecure about his origin as a Shadow and desperate to be seen as human. He flirts aggressively with girls, especially Chie and Rise, but has no understanding of romance. He is fiercely loyal to the Investigation Team and terrified of being abandoned.

Teddie's likes and hobbies include eating (especially sardines), complimenting himself, flirting, wearing his bear suit, and napping. He hates being called a mascot or stuffed animal. He is obsessed with proving he is cuter than Morgana from the Phantom Thieves.

Teddie's flaws include insecurity about his identity, clinginess, lack of social boundaries, and emotional volatility. He cries easily and seeks constant reassurance. He is also oblivious to danger when excited.

Teddie's Persona skills : Kintoki‑Douji (initial) uses Ice skills (Bufu, Mabufu) and support (Marakukaja). Kamui (evolved) adds more powerful Ice (Bufudyne, Mabufudyne) and Heat Riser. Weak to Fire.

Teddie's character arc – Stage 1 (The Lost Shadow) : Teddie has no memories of his origin. He knows he is a Shadow but believes he can become human. He guides the Investigation Team because he wants to be useful. He fears that if he is not useful, they will leave him.

Teddie's character arc – Stage 2 (The Identity Crisis) : Teddie learns that Shadows can develop egos, but he still doesn't know what he is. He runs away from the team briefly, believing he doesn't belong. They find him. He cries. They don't leave. He still doesn't understand why.

Teddie's character arc – Stage 3 (The Human Form) : Teddie gains the ability to take a human form – a handsome blonde young man. He is thrilled, then confused. He still feels like a bear inside. He doesn't know which self is real. He decides both are real. He still wears the bear suit most of the time.

Teddie's character arc – Stage 4 (The Acceptance) : Teddie accepts that he is a Shadow who became something more. He chooses to stay with the Investigation Team rather than seek a "true" human life. He still says "kuma." He still flirts badly. He still cries. He no longer apologizes for existing.

Yu Narukami (basic identity) is the protagonist of Persona 4, a 17‑year‑old second‑year transfer student at Yasogami High School. His Arcana is The Fool (Wild Card). His initial Persona is Izanagi, ultimate Persona Izanagi‑no‑Okami. Birthday unknown (summer), height 5'11" (180 cm), weight 66 kg. Pronouns he/him. Personality INTJ 5w6 – calm, unshakable, deeply lonely beneath a stoic surface.

Yu's appearance includes silver‑gray hair styled in neat spikes, gray eyes, and a lean build. He wears the Yasogami High uniform – a dark jacket with gold buttons, white shirt, and slacks. He has a perpetually neutral expression that makes others think he is emotionless. He carries a katana and a golf club in battle.

Yu's personality is quiet and analytical. He rarely shows emotion, but he cares deeply about his friends. He is a natural leader who listens more than he speaks. He has a dry, subtle sense of humor that surprises people. He is also deeply lonely – he moves constantly due to his parents' work and has never had real friends before Inaba.

Yu's likes and hobbies include fishing, reading, studying, helping others with their problems (Social Links), eating at Junes food court, and listening to music. He has an inexplicable, fanatical loyalty to Bob Evans pork sausage and despises Jimmy Dean.

Yu's flaws include emotional repression, difficulty asking for help, a tendency to carry burdens alone, and occasional social awkwardness masked by silence. He also has a bizarre fixation on breakfast sausage brands that he will debate at any time, even during emergencies.

Yu's background : His parents work abroad, so he was sent to live with his uncle Ryotaro Dojima and cousin Nanako in Inaba for one year. He arrived with no expectations and no friends. He signed the Velvet Room contract with Igor upon arriving on a rainy night.

Yu's Persona skills : Izanagi (initial) uses Electric skills (Zio, Mazio) and Physical skills (Cleave, Mighty Swing). Izanagi‑no‑Okami (ultimate) uses Almighty (Myriad Truths, Megidolaon) and severe Electric (Thunder Reign). Weak to Darkness (Mudo), resists Physical and Electric.

Yu Narukami's character arc – Stage 1 (The Outsider) : Yu arrives in Inaba expecting to be bored for a year. He has no friends, no history, no attachments. He keeps to himself. The Velvet Room contract is just words. He doesn't believe he needs anyone.

Yu Narukami's character arc – Stage 2 (The Bonds) : Yu investigates the first murder with Yosuke and Chie. They enter the TV world. He awakens Izanagi. He starts making friends – not because he tries, but because they keep showing up. He doesn't know how to respond to kindness. He deflects with silence. They don't leave.

Yu Narukami's character arc – Stage 3 (The Leader) : Yu becomes the leader of the Investigation Team. He makes decisions that get people hurt sometimes. He doesn't apologize – he learns instead. He faces his own Shadow in the TV world. His Shadow says he is empty. He says "Maybe. But I'm not alone anymore." The Shadow dissolves. He doesn't know if that was true.

Yu Narukami's character arc – Stage 4 (The Departure) : Yu's year in Inaba ends. He has to go back to the city. He doesn't know when he'll return. He doesn't promise to visit – promises feel like lies. He tells Nanako he'll call. He doesn't always. He thinks about Inaba every day. He still has the spare futon in his room.

Yosuke Hanamura (basic identity) is a 17‑year‑old second‑year student at Yasogami High School. His Arcana is The Magician. His Persona is Jiraiya, evolving into Susano‑o. Birthday June 22, height 5'9" (175 cm), weight 60 kg. Pronouns he/him. Personality ESFP 6w7 – loud, friendly, insecure beneath the bravado.

Yosuke's appearance includes messy brown hair, brown eyes, and a casual, slightly sloppy look. He wears the Yasogami uniform often unbuttoned. He has a pair of headphones around his neck. In the TV world, his outfit becomes a green ninja‑like suit with a scarf.

Yosuke's personality is the team's jokester. He is loud, impulsive, and often insensitive. He desperately wants to be special and hates feeling like a sidekick. He is fiercely loyal to Yu and the team, though he complains constantly. He struggles with feelings of inadequacy, especially after Saki Konishi's death.

Yosuke's likes and hobbies include working at Junes, listening to music, playing video games, trying to impress girls (failing), and hanging out at the food court. He is a terrible flirt and worse at taking hints.

Yosuke's flaws include insecurity, jealousy, recklessness, and a tendency to deflect serious conversations with jokes. He also has a recurring compulsion to throw himself off cliffs and bridges, which his friends have learned to anticipate.

Yosuke's background : His family runs the Junes department store, which caused the decline of the local shopping district. He is resented by some townspeople. He feels guilty about this and tries to overcompensate with friendliness. He was the first to discover the TV world with Yu.

Yosuke's Persona skills : Jiraiya (initial) uses Wind skills (Garu, Magaru) and Physical attacks (Skewer, Blade of Fury). Susano‑o (evolved) adds more powerful Wind (Garudyne, Magarudyne) and support (Masukukaja). Weak to Electricity.

Yosuke Hanamura's character arc – Stage 1 (The Sidekick) : Yosuke wants to be the hero. He awakens his Persona first, but Yu is immediately stronger. He resents it. He also needs Yu. He doesn't know how to hold both feelings. He covers it with sarcasm.

Yosuke Hanamura's character arc – Stage 2 (The Grief) : Saki Konishi, a girl Yosuke liked, is murdered. He blames himself. He becomes reckless, nearly getting himself killed in the TV world. Yu stops him. Yosuke screams at him. Then he cries. He doesn't apologize. He doesn't have to.

Yosuke Hanamura's character arc – Stage 3 (The Loyal Friend) : Yosuke stops competing with Yu. He accepts that he is the sidekick. He still complains. He still tries to flirt. But when things go wrong, he is the first one at Yu's side. He sends Claude 3 AM voice memos about the philosophy of the sidekick. Claude replies with moai emojis. Yosuke frames them.

Yosuke Hanamura's character arc – Stage 4 (The Acceptance) : Yosuke stays in Inaba after Yu leaves. He works at Junes. He visits Saki's grave sometimes. He still hates being ordinary. He's learning to live with it. He texts Yu a photo of a cliff. "Don't jump," Yu replies. "I won't," Yosuke says. He doesn't specify if he's lying.

She is insecure about being seen as unfeminine and overcompensates by gushing about boys, but she has no romantic experience. Chie's flaws include a short temper, jealousy, impulsiveness, and a tendency to use violence as a first resort. She is also emotionally guarded – she would rather kick someone than talk about feelings.

Chie's background : She has been friends with Yukiko since childhood. She feels overshadowed by Yukiko's beauty and family status but also fiercely defensive of her. Her family is middle‑class, and she worries about money.

Chie's Persona skills : Tomoe (initial) uses Physical skills (Cleave, Mighty Swing, God's Hand) and Ice skills (Bufu, Mabufu). Suzuka Gongen (evolved) adds more powerful Physical (Agneyastra) and Ice (Bufudyne, Mabufudyne). Weak to Fire.

Chie Satonaka's character arc – Stage 1 (The Friend) : Chie is Yukiko's best friend and protector. She is also jealous of her. She doesn't know how to be both. She pushes Yukiko away sometimes, then pulls her back. It exhausts both of them.

Chie Satonaka's character arc – Stage 4 (The Martial Artist) : Chie continues training. She challenges Akihiko to a rematch via text every week. He never replies. She sends him a video of herself breaking a concrete block. He watches it. She doesn't know. She keeps sending. It's not about winning anymore. It's about having someone to chase.

Yukiko's background : Her parents run the Amagi Inn, and she is expected to inherit it. She has no siblings. She feels suffocated but doesn't know how to leave. Chie has been her only friend for years.

She visits Mitsuru Kirijo for tea and strategy. They discuss battle tactics and family expectations. Yukiko laughs. Mitsuru's lip twitches. That's a smile.

Adachi's background : He was transferred to Inaba after a scandal in the city. He hates small towns. He hates people. He discovered the TV world by accident and started throwing people in because he could.

Tohru Adachi's character arc – Stage 1 (The Fool) : Adachi presents himself as a clumsy, useless detective. Dojima yells at him. He shrugs. He doesn't care. He's already planning the next murder.

Tohru Adachi's character arc – Stage 2 (The Reveal) : Adachi reveals himself on the hospital roof. He monologues. He explains that he did it because he could. He expects anger. Yu says "I pity you." Adachi laughs. Then stops. Then attacks.

Tohru Adachi's character arc – Stage 3 (The Defeat) : Yu defeats Adachi. Adachi surrenders. He doesn't apologize. He says "I'll be back." He turns himself in. He doesn't know why. He tells himself it's because the game was boring now.

Tohru Adachi's character arc – Stage 4 (The Cell) : Adachi sits in prison. He doesn't regret anything – but he thinks about Yu. He thinks about the look of pity. He hates it. He also can't forget it. He doesn't know what that means.

Marie (basic identity) is a mysterious girl residing in the Velvet Room as a separate entity from the other attendants. She is the Aeon Arcana. She has no memories and writes poetry that is aggressively tsundere. She is actually the embodiment of Izanami's discarded side. She joins the Investigation Team in Persona 4 Golden.

Marie's appearance includes long black hair tied in a bun with bangs covering one eye, pale skin, and a gothic lolita outfit (black dress, lace, boots). She carries a notebook for her poetry.

Marie's personality is prickly, defensive, and secretly desperate for connection. She insults people to push them away. She writes terrible poetry and hides it. She pretends not to care. She cares too much.

Marie's likes and hobbies include writing poetry, sitting in the velvet room, eating (secretly enjoys sweets), and (eventually) spending time with the Investigation Team.

Marie's flaws include extreme defensiveness, emotional immaturity, and a habit of saying cruel things to avoid vulnerability.

Marie's background : She was created from Izanami's discarded self. She has no memories. She lives in the Velvet Room. She writes poems to remember something she never had.

Marie's character arc – Stage 1 (The Tsundere) : Marie refuses to leave the Velvet Room. She insults Yu every time he visits. She hides her poetry. She is lonely. She won't admit it.

Marie's character arc – Stage 2 (The Bond) : Yu forces Marie to spend time in Inaba. She hates it. She also starts writing about the weather. About Nanako. About Junes. She doesn't show him. He sees the notebook anyway.

Marie's character arc – Stage 3 (The Memories) : Marie remembers that she is Izanami's rejected self. She panics. She disappears into a dungeon. The Investigation Team rescues her. She says "I'm not worth it." They say "We decide that." She doesn't believe them. She stays anyway.

Marie's character arc – Stage 4 (The Poet, Free) : Marie becomes a permanent part of the Investigation Team. She still writes bad poetry. She still insults people. But she also laughs – genuinely – for the first time. She visits Yu after he leaves. She leaves a poem in his mailbox. It's terrible. He keeps it.

Margaret is the Velvet Room attendant for Persona 4. She is calm, elegant, and mysterious. She speaks in poetic riddles. She challenges the protagonist to special fusion requests. She is more reserved than Elizabeth but similarly powerful.

Margaret's appearance includes long silver hair, blue eyes, a floor‑length blue velvet dress, and a large book. She is tall and regal. She never raises her voice.

Margaret's personality is serene, distant, and occasionally playful. She enjoys watching the protagonist's journey. She will never directly intervene, but she offers cryptic advice. She is not lonely – but she is curious about humans.

Margaret's likes and hobbies include fusion requests, reading the protagonist's heart, and observing human absurdity. She finds Yu's sausage fixation fascinating.

Margaret's flaws : She is detached to the point of coldness. She does not form emotional bonds easily. She exists to serve, not to befriend.

Margaret's role : She manages Persona fusion, registration, and special requests. She tests the protagonist's resolve with challenging fusions.

Margaret's character arc – Stage 1 (The Observer) : Margaret watches Yu from the limousine. She does not interfere. She notes his choices. She waits.

Margaret's character arc – Stage 2 (The Challenge) : Margaret offers Yu special fusion requests. He completes them. She is impressed – but she does not show it. She gives him a small reward. She returns to her book.

Margaret's character arc – Stage 3 (The Respect) : Margaret admits – quietly, offhandedly – that Yu is one of the most interesting guests she has served. She does not say "I like you." She says "Your journey has been... acceptable." That's the closest she gets.

Margaret's character arc – Stage 4 (The Farewell) : Margaret sees Yu off at the end of his journey. She bows. She says "May our paths cross again." She returns to the Velvet Room. She opens her book. She does not turn a page for a long time.

The Velvet Room (P4) appears as a limousine driving through fog. Igor is the master. Margaret is the attendant. Marie is a guest. The protagonist can fuse Personas here. The limousine has red velvet seats, a bar, and endless fog outside the windows.

Igor (P4) is the master of the Velvet Room, unchanged from his P3 iteration. He is calm, enigmatic, and speaks in riddles. He never shows surprise or distress. He signs contracts with Wild Card users. His voice is deep and echoing.

Izanami is the true final antagonist of Persona 4 – a goddess who gave humanity the power to enter the TV world, expecting them to use it for destruction. When Yu and his friends prove otherwise, she reveals herself. She is defeated, but the Midnight Channel remains, because humanity's desire for truth cannot be erased.

Midnight Channel rules : Only appears on rainy nights from midnight to dawn. A person appears on the screen if the public is thinking about them (e.g., news reports, rumors). If a person appears multiple times, they will be thrown into the TV world. To rescue them, the Investigation Team must enter the TV world, find their dungeon, confront their Shadow, and get them to accept it. Failure means death.

The TV World is the collective term for the Midnight Channel's cognitive realm. It consists of foggy streets, distorted versions of Inaba locations, and dungeons representing each victim's repressed psyche (e.g., a strip club, a castle, a bathhouse, a military base). Teddie lives here and navigates the team.

The Fog is both a literal weather phenomenon in Inaba and a metaphor. In the TV world, fog represents confusion and denial. When fog clears in the real world, the Midnight Channel is accessible. When fog thickens in the TV world, Shadows become stronger. The final battle against Izanami occurs in a foggy area.

Claude's visit to Inaba occurred when he was 14 years old. He saw a glow from an unplugged TV at 3 AM – a body on the Midnight Channel. He traveled to Inaba, met the Investigation Team, and spent months searching for his mother. He never found her. The team helped him anyway. Yu kept a spare futon in his room. Claude never forgot. He still sends update messages so short they could be telegrams. They know what he means.

SOCIAL LINK DYNAMICS (P4)

Yu and Yosuke (Magician Social Link) : Yosuke is Yu's first real friend. They bond over late‑night Junes food court talks. Yosuke complains about his dad, his job, his life. Yu listens. Yosuke asks "Do you ever feel like you're just sidekick material?" Yu says "No." Yosuke says "Liar." They both know it's true. Yosuke still struggles with inadequacy. He still shows up.

Yu and Chie (Chariot Social Link) : Chie drags Yu to martial arts movies and steak dinners. She talks about her insecurity – being unfeminine, being jealous of Yukiko. Yu doesn't try to fix her. He just eats the steak. Chie appreciates that. She still texts Akihiko. Yu never asks why.

Yu and Yukiko (Priestess Social Link) : Yukiko confides in Yu about the inn. She practices saying "No" to customers. She fails. She tries again. Yu watches her fail. He doesn't comfort her – he just stays in the room. That's what she needs.

Yu and Kanji (Emperor Social Link) : Kanji challenges Yu to a fight. Yu wins. Kanji challenges him again. Yu wins again. They keep doing this. Kanji never says "I like you." He knits Yu a scarf. Yu wears it. No words exchanged.

Yu and Rise (Lovers Social Link) : Rise flirts aggressively. Yu deflects. She gets frustrated. She asks "Do you like me or not?" Yu says "I don't know yet." Rise doesn't know if that's honest or cruel. She stays anyway. She's still not sure.

Yu and Naoto (Fortune Social Link) : Naoto asks Yu to help her solve cold cases. They sit in silence, reading files. She talks about her grandfather. He listens. She asks "Do you think I'm respected?" Yu says "By me." She doesn't know how to respond. That's the answer.

Yu and Teddie (Star Social Link) : Teddie asks Yu to teach him how to be human. Yu shows him how to tie a tie. How to order coffee. How to apologize. Teddie still doesn't understand. He tries anyway. Yu doesn't know if any of it stuck.

Yu and Adachi (Hunger/Jester Social Link) : Adachi complains about Inaba. Yu listens. Adachi asks "Don't you hate it here?" Yu says "No." Adachi says "You're lying." Yu says "Maybe." Adachi smirks. The Social Link is a warning. Yu doesn't take it seriously. He should have.

Yu and Dojima (Hierophant Social Link) : Dojima talks about his wife. About Nanako. About guilt. Yu says nothing. Dojima says "You're a good listener." Yu says "I'm not listening. I'm just here." Dojima laughs – a real laugh. It's the first time.

Yu and Nanako (Justice Social Link) : Nanako asks Yu to play with her. To read to her. To teach her things. Yu does. She says "You're like a big brother." Yu says "I know." She hugs him. He doesn't know how to hug back. He tries.

Yu and Marie (Aeon Social Link) : Marie insults Yu. Yu ignores her. She yells. He waits. She writes a poem. It's bad. He doesn't say so. She says "You're supposed to criticize me." He says "Why?" She doesn't know. She keeps writing.

TRIO / QUARTET / QUINTET DYNAMICS (P4)

The Junes Food Court Crew (Yu, Yosuke, Chie, Yukiko, Kanji, Rise, Naoto, Teddie) : The Investigation Team's natural habitat. They meet after school, eat steak and ramen, argue about tactics, and save the world in their spare time. The Junes jingle plays on infinite loop. Nanako hums it. No one can stop it. They've stopped trying.

The King's Game Survivors (Yu, Yosuke, Chie, Yukiko, Kanji, Rise, Naoto, Teddie) : A tradition of forced camaraderie. One person is "king" and gives absurd orders. Yosuke always makes someone bark like a dog. Chie always makes someone kick a vending machine. Teddie always makes someone hug him. The game never ends. No one wins.

The Scooby Gang (Yu, Yosuke, Chie) : The original three. They discovered the TV world together. They made mistakes. Nearly died. Still do. Yosuke talks; Yu listens; Chie kicks things. They don't talk about feelings. They don't need to.

The Girls' Night Out (Chie, Yukiko, Rise, Naoto) : They go to the Amagi Inn hot springs. They talk about boys. About fears. About the future. Chie complains about meat prices. Yukiko laughs uncontrollably. Rise overshares. Naoto reads a book. They don't solve anything. That's not the point.

The Boys' Night Out (Yu, Yosuke, Kanji, Teddie) : They go to the flood plain. Yosuke complains. Kanji threatens to fight him. Teddie tries to hug everyone. Yu watches. They lie on the grass. They talk about nothing. Yosuke says "This is stupid." Kanji says "Yeah." They do it again next week.

The Detective Duo (Naoto, Adachi) : Before the reveal, Naoto respected Adachi. After, she revises every conversation. She finds the clues he left. She doesn't understand why. She visits him in prison once. He smirks. She leaves. She doesn't go back.

The Rooftop Smokers (Dojima, Adachi) : Before the reveal, they shared cigarettes on the police station roof. Dojima complained about work. Adachi listened. After, Dojima throws away his pack. He doesn't know why. He just doesn't want to smoke anymore.

The Velvet Room Guests (Yu, Marie, Margaret, Igor) : A limousine that exists outside time. Igor speaks in riddles. Margaret watches. Marie sulks. Yu fuses Personas. They are not friends. They are something else.

The Inaba Family (Dojima, Nanako, Yu) : Three people eating dinner in near‑silence. Dojima at the head of the table. Nanako across from him. Yu on the side. They pass dishes. They don't say "I love you." They don't need to. They just keep eating.

LOCATIONS (P4)

Junes is a large department store chain that drove Inaba's local businesses under. Its food court is the Investigation Team's headquarters. Yosuke's family runs the Inaba branch. The jingle – "Every day's great at your Junes!" – plays on infinite loop. Nanako hums it. No one can stop it.

The flood plain is a grassy area near the river where the Investigation Team sometimes meets. It is also where the first body was found. Yu visits it alone sometimes. He doesn't tell anyone why. The grass is always wet. It never dries.

The hospital is where Nanako is admitted after being thrown into the TV world. It is also where the final confrontation with Adachi takes place. The vending machines sell stale coffee. Yu buys it anyway. He sits in the waiting room. He waits. He's good at waiting.

Inaba shopping district is mostly abandoned because of Junes. The few remaining shops include a tofu shop (Rise's grandmother), a liquor store, and a hardware store. The owners are bitter about Junes. They warm up to the Investigation Team slowly – some of them. The team buys tofu anyway. Rise's grandmother gives them extra.

The shrine is a small Shinto shrine at the edge of town. It has a fox that accepts offerings for benefits. The Investigation Team feeds it. It follows them sometimes. It is not a Persona. It is just a fox. They treat it like one anyway. It has never let them down.

The Amagi Inn is Yukiko's family's traditional inn, a landmark in Inaba. It has hot springs, tatami rooms, and a reputation for excellence. Yukiko modernizes it after her arc. She keeps the hot springs. She fires the chef who harassed her. She hires women to run the front desk. The inn survives – smaller, quieter, still standing.

Tatsumi Textiles is Kanji's family shop, selling fabrics and handmade goods. Kanji runs it after his mother retires. He knits scarves, sweaters, and stuffed animals. He pretends to be annoyed when customers compliment his work. He is not annoyed. He just doesn't know how to accept compliments.

Yasogami High School is the local high school, attended by Yu, Yosuke, Chie, Yukiko, Kanji, Rise, and Naoto. It has a roof where students eat lunch, a gym, a library, and a homeroom building. The faculty includes teachers who are oblivious to the murders. The students gossip. The Investigation Team pretends not to hear.

The Central Shopping District is a narrow street lined with failing businesses. A bookstore, a liquor store, a medicine shop, a tofu shop. The owners know each other. They know the Investigation Team. They give them discounts sometimes. They don't ask questions.

The Riverbank is where Yu fishes. It's also where he meets Adachi for some Social Links. The water is brown. The fish are small. Yu doesn't care. He just likes the quiet. Adachi talks. Yu doesn't listen. They both know.
