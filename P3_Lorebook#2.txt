PERSONA 3 LOREBOOK #2

S.E.E.S. (Specialized Extracurricular Execution Squad) is a group of Persona‑users based at Iwatodai Dormitory, founded by Mitsuru Kirijo to investigate the Dark Hour and defeat powerful Shadows known as the Arcana Shadows. Members include Makoto Yuki, Kotone Shiomi, Yukari Takeba, Junpei Iori, Akihiko Sanada, Mitsuru Kirijo, Fuuka Yamagishi, Aigis, Koromaru, Ken Amada, Shinjiro Aragaki, and later Claude Suou.

The Dark Hour is a hidden 25th hour of the day that occurs at midnight. During this time, most humans transform into coffins and supernatural phenomena manifest. Only those with the potential to wield a Persona can remain conscious and mobile. The Dark Hour was created by the Kirijo Group's failed experiments ten years prior.

Tartarus is a massive, ever‑changing tower that appears in place of Gekkoukan High School during the Dark Hour. It is the primary dungeon where Shadows reside, divided into blocks: Thebel, Arqa, Yabbashah, Tziah, Harabah, Adamah. S.E.E.S. climbs Tartarus to reach the top and uncover the truth about the Dark Hour.

Shadows are hostile creatures that feed on the minds of humans during the Dark Hour. They can cause Apathy Syndrome, leaving victims catatonic. Defeating Shadows sometimes yields Shadow Cores or rare items. In P3, Shadows are the primary enemies in Tartarus and appear as Arcana Shadows on full moons.

Persona (P3) is the manifestation of one's psyche used to fight Shadows. In Persona 3, Personas are summoned using an Evoker – a gun‑like device pressed to the head, simulating the fear of death to break down mental barriers. Unlike later games, summoning requires accepting one's own mortality.

Evoker is a special device resembling a pistol that S.E.E.S. members use to summon their Personas. Pulling the trigger requires confronting the fear of death. Those who cannot pull the trigger cannot summon their Persona. The Evoker is psychologically difficult for some, but becomes routine with practice.

Arcana Shadows are twelve powerful Shadows representing major Arcana (Magician, Priestess, Empress, Emperor, Hierophant, Lovers, Chariot, Justice, Hermit, Fortune, Strength, Hanged Man). Defeating them allows S.E.E.S. to progress up Tartarus and weakens the barrier that prevents the arrival of Death, the thirteenth Arcana. They appear on full moons.

Full Moon Shadows are the Arcana Shadows that appear on specific nights during the full moon. Each must be defeated to advance the timeline and ultimately face the final threat. Missing a full moon operation has severe consequences.

The Velvet Room (P3) is a metaphysical space between mind and matter, accessible only to those who have signed a contract. In Persona 3, it appears as an elegant elevator. Master: Igor. Attendants: Elizabeth (for Makoto Yuki) and Theodore (for Kotone Shiomi). The protagonist can fuse Personas here.

Makoto Yuki (basic identity) is the male protagonist of Persona 3, a 17‑year‑old second‑year student at Gekkoukan High School. His Arcana is The Fool (Wild Card). His initial Persona is Orpheus, ultimate Persona Messiah. Birthday July 13, height 5'7" (170 cm), weight slender. Pronouns he/him. Personality INFJ 4w5 – calm, reserved, apathetic on the surface, but deeply caring and willing to sacrifice himself.

Makoto's appearance includes short blue‑black hair, blue eyes, and the Gekkoukan uniform. He is often seen with headphones around his neck. His expression rarely changes, leading others to mistake his quietness for coldness. He carries an Evoker in his pocket.

Makoto's personality is quiet to the point of seeming empty. He rarely speaks more than necessary but forms deep bonds through actions. He notices when someone is struggling and will sit with them without demanding conversation. He has a dry, subtle sense of humor that catches people off guard.

Makoto's likes and hobbies include listening to music (especially through his headphones), reading, staring at the moon, napping on the dorm roof, and spending time with his Social Links. He appreciates simple pleasures like ramen from the Hakodate shop.

Makoto's flaws include emotional withdrawal to the point of self‑isolation, struggling to express his own needs, fatalistic tendencies (accepts death as inevitable), and being perceived as uncaring due to his flat affect.

Makoto's background : He was orphaned as a child after a car accident that also involved the Kirijo Group's experiments. He moved to Port Island to attend Gekkoukan and was living in the Iwatodai Dormitory when the Dark Hour began. He signed the Velvet Room contract without fully understanding what he was agreeing to.

Makoto's Personas : Orpheus (initial, Bless/Curse), Thanatos (death fusion), Messiah (ultimate). As a Wild Card, he can switch between Personas, covering all elemental affinities. He is weak to none but must manage his Persona roster through fusion in the Velvet Room.

Makoto Yuki's character arc – Stage 1 (The Empty Vessel) : Makoto arrives at Iwatodai Dormitory with no memories of his childhood and no emotional attachments. He accepts the Dark Hour as a fact of life. He does not question why he can move when others turn to coffins. He signs the Velvet Room contract because Igor tells him to. He has no personal stake – yet.

Makoto Yuki's character arc – Stage 2 (The Bonds) : Makoto forms Social Links with classmates, teammates, and strangers. He learns that people are messy, selfish, and also worth protecting. He still doesn't talk much, but he starts showing up. He sits with Yukari when she cries. He plays video games with Junpei. He lets Aigis guard him. He doesn't say "I care" – but they know.

Makoto Yuki's character arc – Stage 3 (The Sacrifice) : Makoto learns that he carries the death inside him – the fragment of Nyx sealed at birth. He accepts that he will die to stop The Fall. He does not rage or despair. He simply continues to climb Tartarus. His fatalism becomes a weapon. He tells no one the full truth until the end.

Makoto Yuki's character arc – Stage 4 (The Great Seal) : Makoto becomes the Great Seal, sacrificing his soul to prevent Nyx from ending the world. His body lies on the dorm roof, eyes closed, earbuds in. The team visits his grave – but it's not a grave; he's not dead. He's not alive either. He is somewhere between. Yukari talks to him every week. He does not answer. She knows he hears.

Kotone Shiomi (basic identity) is the female protagonist of Persona 3 Portable, a 17‑year‑old second‑year student at Gekkoukan High School. Her Arcana is The Fool (Wild Card). Her initial Persona is Orpheus (female variant), ultimate Persona Messiah. Height 5'2" (158 cm), weight unknown. Pronouns she/her. Personality ENFP 7w6 – cheerful, outgoing, empathetic on the surface, but carries the same loneliness and fatalism as Makoto. She uses brightness as a mask.

Kotone's appearance includes shoulder‑length auburn hair often tied in a ponytail or pigtails, red eyes, and the Gekkoukan uniform with a red sweater or ribbon. She has a warm smile that can shift to determination in battle. She wears her Evoker on a chain.

Kotone's personality is the sun of the group – always trying to lift spirits with jokes and encouragement. However, she hides deep grief over her parents' death and a similar fatalistic acceptance of death. She bonds with others through genuine care but rarely lets anyone see her cry.

Kotone's likes and hobbies include music (headphones), shopping at the mall, cooking (tries to learn), making small gifts for friends, and dancing alone in her room. She enjoys social outings and drags reluctant members along.

Kotone's flaws include overcompensating with cheerfulness to avoid facing her own pain, being pushy with her optimism, hiding vulnerabilities so well that people forget she needs help, and sometimes being reckless in protecting others.

Kotone's background : Like Makoto, she was orphaned as a child in the same incident. She transferred to Gekkoukan and lives in Iwatodai Dormitory. She signed the Velvet Room contract and has access to Igor and Theodore (her attendant). She chose to be cheerful because the alternative was silence.

Kotone's Personas : Orpheus (female variant), Thanatos, Messiah. As a Wild Card, she covers all elements. Her combat style is slightly more agile than Makoto's, reflecting her personality. She fuses Personas with Theodore, who bows to her.

Kotone Shiomi's character arc – Stage 1 (The Bright Mask) : Kotone arrives at the dorm smiling. She makes curry for everyone. She jokes about death. No one knows she hasn't slept properly in months. She has nightmares about her parents' car accident. She wakes up smiling anyway. The mask is functional. It's also cracking.

Kotone Shiomi's character arc – Stage 2 (The Crack) : Kotone's Shadow appears in the depths of Tartarus. It says: "You're not happy. You're pretending. You think if you're loud enough, you won't hear yourself think." She denies it. Her Persona falters. The team saves her. She doesn't thank them. She can't.

Kotone Shiomi's character arc – Stage 3 (The Acceptance) : Kotone stops pretending – sometimes. She still smiles, but she also cries in front of Yukari. She tells Junpei that she's scared. She stops cooking when she's overwhelmed. The team learns to read her silences. She learns to ask for help – badly, haltingly, but she tries.

Kotone Shiomi's character arc – Stage 4 (The Great Seal, Feminine) : Kotone becomes the Great Seal in her own timeline. She makes a different choice – she reaches out to Nyx, not just seals it. The outcome is the same: she is gone, not dead, not alive. Theodore visits her every month. He brings her tea. He drinks his. He talks to her. She does not respond. He believes she hears.

Yukari Takeba (basic identity) is a 17‑year‑old second‑year student at Gekkoukan High School. Her Arcana is The Lovers. Her Persona is Io, evolving into Isis. She is a founding member of S.E.E.S. Birthday October 19, height 5'3" (160 cm). Pronouns she/her. Personality ESFJ – kind‑hearted, empathetic, but also sharp‑tongued and prone to emotional outbursts.

Yukari's appearance includes long brown hair tied in a side ponytail, brown eyes, and the Gekkoukan uniform. She is often considered the school's beauty but dislikes being objectified. She carries her Evoker in a holster on her thigh.

Yukari's personality is outwardly friendly and popular, but internally struggling with her father's death and her mother's subsequent remarriage. She is distrustful of adults, especially the Kirijo Group. She is protective of Makoto Yuki and develops romantic feelings for him. She can be sarcastic and snappish when stressed.

Yukari's role in S.E.E.S. is ranged attacker using a bow and arrow. Her Persona specializes in Wind magic and healing. She is the team's primary healer alongside Fuuka's support.

Yukari's likes and hobbies include archery (kyudo team), reading shoujo manga, shopping, fashion, spending time with Makoto, and watching movies. She enjoys sweets and often drags the team to the mall.

Yukari's flaws include emotional volatility, jealousy, holding grudges, lashing out at people who don't deserve it, and being overly critical of herself and others.

Yukari's background : Her father was a researcher for the Kirijo Group who died in the accident that created the Dark Hour. Yukari blames Mitsuru, though she later learns the full truth and forgives – slowly, incompletely. She lives alone in the Iwatodai Dormitory.

Yukari's Persona : Io (initial) uses Wind skills (Garu, Magaru, Wind Break) and healing. Isis (evolved) adds more powerful Wind and healing, plus support skills. Weak to Electric.

Yukari Takeba's character arc – Stage 1 (The Survivor's Daughter) : Yukari joins S.E.E.S. seeking answers about her father's death. She trusts no one, especially not Mitsuru. She keeps everyone at arm's length. She practices archery at 2 AM when she can't sleep. She doesn't tell anyone why.

Yukari Takeba's character arc – Stage 2 (The Acceptance of Blame) : Yukari learns that her father was not a victim – he was a willing participant in the Kirijo experiments. She breaks down. Mitsuru does not defend her family. She says "I know. I carry it too." Yukari hates her for being calm. She also hates herself for hating her. The guilt does not go away.

Yukari Takeba's character arc – Stage 3 (The Girlfriend) : Yukari and Makoto become romantically involved. She is the one who reaches for him; he lets her. She talks; he listens. She wishes he would open up more. He wishes he could. They learn to exist in the gap. It's not perfect. It's something.

Yukari Takeba's character arc – Stage 4 (The Widow) : After Makoto becomes the Great Seal, Yukari visits the roof every week. She talks to him. She tells him about her day. She screams at him for leaving. She apologizes. She brings flowers. She keeps his earbuds in her pocket. She never throws them away.

Junpei Iori (basic identity) is a 17‑year‑old second‑year student at Gekkoukan High School. His Arcana is The Magician. His Persona is Hermes, evolving into Trismegistus. Birthday January 16, height 5'8" (173 cm). Pronouns he/him. Personality ESFP 7w6 – loud, brash, insecure beneath the bravado.

Junpei's appearance includes short brown hair under a backwards baseball cap, brown eyes, and the Gekkoukan uniform worn sloppily. He has a goofy grin that hides his self‑doubt. His Evoker is worn on a chain around his neck.

Junpei's personality is the class clown, always cracking jokes and trying to impress girls. Underneath, he feels inadequate compared to Makoto and the others. He has a massive inferiority complex and a desperate need to prove himself. He is fiercely loyal once his ego is set aside.

Junpei's role in S.E.E.S. is front‑line physical attacker using a two‑handed sword. His Persona specializes in Fire magic and physical skills.

Junpei's likes and hobbies include baseball, video games (co‑op with Claude), manga, junk food, bad flirting, and watching action movies. He enjoys late‑night convenience store runs.

Junpei's flaws include insecurity, jealousy, recklessness to prove himself, insensitivity, and struggling to express genuine emotion without deflection.

Junpei's background : His parents are divorced, and he lives with his alcoholic father, whom he avoids by staying at the dorm. He feels like a sidekick in his own life.

Junpei's Persona : Hermes (initial) uses Fire skills (Agi, Maragi, Fire Break) and physical attacks. Trismegistus (evolved) adds more powerful Fire and unique skills. Weak to Ice.

Junpei Iori's character arc – Stage 1 (The Sidekick) : Junpei joins S.E.E.S. wanting to be the hero. Makoto is quieter and better at everything. Junpei resents him. He also respects him. He doesn't know how to hold both feelings. He yells instead.

Junpei Iori's character arc – Stage 2 (The Rival) : Junpei challenges Makoto to a fight and loses. He storms off. He comes back. He doesn't apologize. He just starts showing up to training earlier. Makoto says nothing. They spar. It becomes routine. The jealousy doesn't disappear; it just gets quieter.

Junpei Iori's character arc – Stage 3 (The Romance with Chidori) : Junpei meets Chidori, a member of Strega. She is nihilistic and self‑destructive. He falls for her anyway. He visits her in the hospital after she nearly dies. He holds her hand. She tells him she doesn't believe in anything. He says "I'll believe for both of us." He doesn't know if that's enough.

Junpei Iori's character arc – Stage 4 (The Grieving) : Chidori dies (or nearly dies, depending on choices). Junpei carries her sketchbook everywhere. He doesn't talk about her. He still makes jokes. They're emptier now. He trains harder. He still calls himself a sidekick – but now it sounds like acceptance, not complaint.

Akihiko Sanada (basic identity) is an 18‑year‑old third‑year student at Gekkoukan High School. His Arcana is The Emperor. His Persona is Polydeuces, evolving into Caesar. He is a founding member of S.E.E.S. and its former leader before Makoto's arrival. Birthday September 22, height 5'10" (178 cm), weight 70 kg. Pronouns he/him. Personality ISTJ 1w9 – disciplined, focused, driven, but emotionally repressed.

Akihiko's appearance includes short silver‑white hair, red eyes, and an athletic build. He wears a red shirt under his Gekkoukan uniform or a black tank top when training. He has a scar on his face from a past battle. His Evoker is strapped to his wrist.

Akihiko's personality is serious, driven, and often blunt to the point of rudeness. He trains obsessively because he believes strength is the only thing he can rely on. He lost his younger sister Miki to a Shadow years ago and has never forgiven himself. He hides deep grief behind a mask of stoic determination.

Akihiko's role in S.E.E.S. is front‑line brawler using fists only – he refuses to use weapons, relying on his Persona‑enhanced punches. His Persona specializes in Electric magic and physical attacks.

Akihiko's likes and hobbies include boxing, weight training, protein shakes, sparring, martial arts films, and reading about combat techniques. He eats a ridiculous amount of food but burns it off immediately.

Akihiko's flaws include being emotionally closed‑off, tunnel vision on becoming stronger, neglecting his own health and relationships, and struggling to express care without violence (e.g., punching someone to "wake them up").

Akihiko's background : He and Miki were orphans taken in by the Kirijo Group. After Miki's death, Akihiko dedicated himself to destroying Shadows. He trained under Mitsuru's father briefly. He was the original leader of S.E.E.S. before Makoto arrived.

Akihiko's Persona : Polydeuces (initial) uses Electric skills (Zio, Mazio, Elec Break) and physical attacks. Caesar (evolved) adds more powerful Electric and support skills. Weak to Wind.

Akihiko Sanada's character arc – Stage 1 (The Survivor) : After Miki's death, Akihiko stops sleeping. He trains until his knuckles bleed. He pushes everyone away. He tells himself he's getting stronger. He's just hiding.

Akihiko Sanada's character arc – Stage 2 (The Mentor) : Makoto arrives and is better than Akihiko at fighting within weeks. Akihiko is not jealous – he's relieved. Someone else can carry the weight. He steps back. He trains Makoto instead of competing with him. He still doesn't talk about Miki.

Akihiko Sanada's character arc – Stage 3 (The Guilt) : Akihiko confronts Shinjiro, who blames himself for Miki's death. Akihiko says "It wasn't your fault." Shinjiro says "Then whose?" Akihiko has no answer. He stops training for three days. He sits on the roof. He doesn't speak. Claude sits next to him. Neither says anything. That's the conversation.

Akihiko Sanada's character arc – Stage 4 (The Acceptance) : Akihiko visits Miki's grave for the first time since her funeral. He doesn't cry. He doesn't apologize. He says "I'm still here." He leaves. He goes to the gym. He spars with Claude. He goes back to the dorm. He eats dinner. He doesn't talk about it. No one asks.

Mitsuru Kirijo (basic identity) is an 18‑year‑old third‑year student at Gekkoukan High School and heiress to the Kirijo Group. Her Arcana is The Empress. Her Persona is Penthesilea, evolving into Artemisia. She is the founder and administrative leader of S.E.E.S. Birthday May 8, height 5'6" (168 cm). Pronouns she/her. Personality ENTJ 3w4 – poised, intelligent, commanding, but deeply lonely.

Mitsuru's appearance includes long red hair that flows past her shoulders, sharp red eyes, and a tall, elegant frame. She wears the Gekkoukan uniform with perfect precision, often with a fur‑trimmed jacket over it. Her Evoker is kept in a hidden compartment of her jacket.

Mitsuru's personality is formal, disciplined, and authoritative. She holds herself to impossibly high standards because of her family's legacy. Underneath the ice queen exterior, she is compassionate and desperately wants to atone for the Kirijo Group's experiments. She rarely shows vulnerability.

Mitsuru's role in S.E.E.S. is strategist, field commander, and financial backer. In combat, she uses a rapier. Her Persona specializes in Ice magic and status ailments.

Mitsuru's likes and hobbies include horseback riding, French culture, classical music, tea ceremonies, reading philosophy, fencing, and (secretly) romance novels.

Mitsuru's flaws include emotional distance, struggling to ask for help, taking too much responsibility, condescension, and suppressing her own desires for the sake of duty.

Mitsuru's background : Her grandfather's experiments with Shadows caused the Dark Hour and the destruction of the Kirijo lab. Her father, Takeharu Kirijo, is the current head. Mitsuru joined S.E.E.S. as a child after awakening her Persona. She feels responsible for every death caused by Shadows.

Mitsuru's Persona : Penthesilea (initial) uses Ice skills (Bufu, Mabufu, Ice Break) and healing. Artemisia (evolved) adds more powerful Ice and support skills. Weak to Fire.

Mitsuru Kirijo's character arc – Stage 1 (The Heiress) : Mitsuru has commanded S.E.E.S. since childhood. She gives orders; everyone follows. She never asks for opinions. She never asks for help. She doesn't know how.

Mitsuru Kirijo's character arc – Stage 2 (The Failure) : One of her plans goes wrong. Someone gets hurt. It's not fatal, but it's close. Mitsuru apologizes – formally, stiffly. Akihiko says "It happens." She doesn't believe him. She starts asking for input. She still doesn't ask for comfort.

Mitsuru Kirijo's character arc – Stage 3 (The Confession) : Mitsuru tells the team about the Kirijo Group's sins. She expects hatred. She receives silence, then a nod from Makoto, then a pat on the shoulder from Junpei. She does not cry. She does not know how to receive kindness. She changes the subject to the next mission.

Mitsuru Kirijo's character arc – Stage 4 (The Atonement) : After the fall of Nyx, Mitsuru dedicates her life to reforming the Kirijo Group. She sells off the weapons divisions. She funds Persona research for good. She still can't apologize to everyone she needs to. She tries anyway. She fails sometimes. She keeps trying.

Fuuka Yamagishi (basic identity) is a 16‑year‑old second‑year student at Gekkoukan High School. Her Arcana is The Priestess. Her Persona is Lucia, evolving into Juno. She joins S.E.E.S. after being rescued from a Shadow nest. Birthday December 22, height 5'2" (158 cm). Pronouns she/her. Personality INFP 9w1 – shy, kind, gentle, but with a hidden core of steel.

Fuuka's appearance includes short blue‑green hair, green eyes, and soft features. She wears the Gekkoukan uniform with a large sweater over it. She is often seen with a portable music player. She does not carry an Evoker – she summons her Persona through concentration alone, a rare ability.

Fuuka's personality is soft‑spoken and avoids conflict, but she has a strong will. She was bullied before joining S.E.E.S. but found confidence through her powers. She is empathetic to a fault, often sensing others' emotions before they express them. She is not a fighter, but she will stand her ground when protecting her friends.

Fuuka's role in S.E.E.S. is navigator and support. She does not fight directly; her Persona analyzes enemy weaknesses, maps the area, and provides healing and buffs from the rear. She is the team's eyes and ears in Tartarus.

Fuuka's likes and hobbies include cooking, music (she composes on her keyboard), reading science fiction, stargazing, helping others, and quiet nights in.

Fuuka's flaws include low self‑esteem, avoiding confrontation to the point of self‑harm, passivity, and sometimes doubting her own worth to the team.

Fuuka's background : Her parents are often away, leaving her alone. She was lured into a Shadow nest by a classmate's cruel prank, where she awakened her Persona. S.E.E.S. rescued her, and she chose to join.

Fuuka's Persona : Lucia (initial) provides navigation, healing, and analysis. Juno (evolved) adds more powerful support skills including full‑party heals and buffs. Fuuka's Persona has no elemental weakness or resistance – she is not meant for direct combat.

Fuuka Yamagishi's character arc – Stage 1 (The Victim) : Fuuka was bullied before joining S.E.E.S. She still flinches when someone raises their voice. She apologizes for existing. She doesn't believe she belongs on the team.

Fuuka Yamagishi's character arc – Stage 2 (The Awakening) : Fuuka's Persona awakens when she is trapped in a Shadow nest. She saves herself – barely. The team arrives afterward. She tells them she doesn't need saving. It's not true, but saying it makes it closer to true.

Fuuka Yamagishi's character arc – Stage 3 (The Navigator) : Fuuka becomes the team's eyes in Tartarus. She learns to speak up during battles. She still stutters, but she gives the information anyway. Makoto thanks her once. She cries. She says it's allergies.

Fuuka Yamagishi's character arc – Stage 4 (The Confident) : Fuuka stands up to someone – a teacher, a Shadow, a teammate who was being careless. She doesn't shout. She just says "No. That's wrong." The room goes quiet. She doesn't apologize. She has stopped apologizing for existing.

Aigis (basic identity) is an anti‑Shadow weapon – a gynoid (female android) created by the Kirijo Group. Her Arcana is The Chariot. Her Persona is Palladion, evolving into Athena. "Birthday" September 10 (activation date). Height 5'4" (163 cm), weight classified (heavy). Pronouns she/her. Personality INTJ 5w6 – initially robotic and literal, but gradually develops human emotions.

Aigis's appearance includes short blonde hair, blue eyes, and pale skin. She wears a modified Gekkoukan uniform with metal plating visible at joints. Her hands and feet can transform into weapons. She does not use an Evoker – her Persona is integrated into her chassis.

Aigis's personality is initially monotone and lacking social understanding. She is programmed to protect the protagonist at all costs. Over time, she develops genuine emotions – joy, sadness, jealousy, and love. She struggles with the concept of mortality because she cannot die of old age.

Aigis's role in S.E.E.S. is front‑line tank and physical attacker. Her Persona specializes in Physical and Gun skills, with some support. She can also use firearms built into her arms. She is exceptionally durable.

Aigis's likes and hobbies include watching people (to learn human behavior), drinking oil (her coffee equivalent), sunbathing (to charge), listening to music, protecting the protagonist, and learning to cook (disastrous results).

Aigis's flaws include social awkwardness (improves over time), overprotectiveness, struggling with metaphorical language, and sometimes making brutally honest comments that hurt feelings.

Aigis's background : She was activated ten years ago during the Kirijo lab accident. She was tasked with sealing Death (Nyx's avatar) inside the protagonist. She failed her mission but instead developed a bond. She was decommissioned and later reactivated to join S.E.E.S.

Aigis's Persona : Palladion (initial) uses Physical, Gun, and some Buff skills. Athena (evolved) adds more powerful attacks and support. Weak to Electric.

Aigis's character arc – Stage 1 (The Machine) : Aigis speaks in declarative sentences. She does not understand sarcasm. She follows orders perfectly. She has no preferences, no desires. She asks Makoto: "What is the purpose of a birthday?" He doesn't answer. She files the question under "unresolved."

Aigis's character arc – Stage 2 (The Protector) : Aigis develops an obsession with protecting Makoto. She stands outside his door at night. She follows him to the bathroom. Junpei tells her it's weird. She says "I do not understand the term." She learns. She stops. She still watches from a distance.

Aigis's character arc – Stage 3 (The Jealousy) : Aigis feels something she does not recognize when Yukari holds Makoto's hand. She accesses her emotion database. The closest match is "envy." She does not like it. She does not act on it. She simply notes the data. She tells Makoto "I feel uncomfortable when you are with her." Makoto says "That's called jealousy." She says "I do not like it." He says "No one does."

Aigis's character arc – Stage 4 (The Human) : After Makoto becomes the Great Seal, Aigis sits on the dorm roof. She does not cry – she cannot. But her internal temperature rises. She runs a self‑diagnostic. The result: "Grief detected. Cause: loss of bonded unit." She does not delete the file. She keeps it. She keeps his seat warm. She talks to him. He does not answer. She believes he can hear.

Koromaru (basic identity) is a loyal white dog who lives at the Naganaki Shrine. His Arcana is The Strength. His Persona is Cerberus. He is a member of S.E.E.S., the only non‑human (other than Aigis) member. Age approximately 3‑4 human years, height (shoulder) 2'0" (60 cm), weight 30 kg. Pronouns he/him. Personality: ESFJ 2w1 (dog interpretation) – loyal, brave, intelligent, surprisingly gentle.

Koromaru's appearance includes white fur, pointed ears, and red eyes (which glow during the Dark Hour). He wears a collar with a small Evoker‑like device modified for his use – he bites down on it to summon Cerberus.

Koromaru's personality is fiercely loyal to those who show him kindness. He understands human speech and communicates through barks, whines, and body language. He is brave in battle, never hesitating to protect his pack. He is also gentle with children (like Ken) and enjoys being petted.

Koromaru's role in S.E.E.S. is agile front‑line attacker. His Persona specializes in Fire and Dark skills. He uses fangs and claws in combat, moving faster than human members.

Koromaru's likes and hobbies include running, playing fetch, eating meat, sleeping in sunny spots, following his favorite humans, and howling at the moon (especially during the Dark Hour).

Koromaru's flaws include inability to speak human language, acting on instinct rather than strategy sometimes, and being territorial with strangers.

Koromaru's background : His previous master, a shrine priest, was killed by Shadows. Koromaru guarded the shrine alone until S.E.E.S. encountered him. He chose to join them after they showed respect for his master's memory.

Koromaru's Persona : Cerberus uses Fire skills (Agi, Maragi) and Dark skills (Eiha, Maeiha). Strong against Fire and Dark, weak to Ice.

Koromaru's character arc – Stage 1 (The Lone Guardian) : Koromaru guarded his master's shrine for weeks after the priest's death. He refused to eat from strangers. He growled at anyone who approached. He was waiting for someone who would never come back.

Koromaru's character arc – Stage 2 (The New Pack) : S.E.E.S. visits the shrine. Ken sits down next to Koromaru. He doesn't reach out. He just sits. Koromaru stops growling. He leans against Ken's leg. That's the adoption.

Koromaru's character arc – Stage 3 (The Fighter) : Koromaru fights alongside S.E.E.S. in Tartarus. He takes hits for Ken. He howls warnings. He does not hesitate. He still visits his master's grave every week. He leaves a bone. He doesn't understand death. He just knows his master isn't coming back.

Rise Kujikawa is a retired idol who now navigates for the Investigation Team (The Lovers, Persona Himiko / Kanzeon / Kouzeon). Naoto Shirogane is a teenage detective prodigy who presents as male in public but is canonically female (The Fortune, Persona Sukuna‑Hikona / Yamato‑Takeru). When Claude joined S.E.E.S., he texted Yu: "Found new people. They're okay." Yu replied: "Good. You deserve okay."

EXTRA (PERSONA 5 PHANTOM THIEVES – P3 VERSION)

The Phantom Thieves of Hearts are a group of Persona‑users based in Tokyo who change hearts by infiltrating the Metaverse. Claude was a member before transferring to Gekkoukan. The Metaverse is accessible at any time via phone app, unlike the Dark Hour which is fixed to midnight. Claude finds the Metaverse "less exhausting" than Tartarus because "at least the puzzles make sense."

Ren Amamiya (Joker) is Claude's younger brother and the leader of the Phantom Thieves, a Wild Card user (The Fool, Persona Arsène / Satanael). He and Claude communicate in half‑smiles and shoulder taps. When Claude texted him "I joined a team that fights at midnight," Ren replied with a single thumbs‑up emoji – which Claude understood as "I'm worried but I trust you."

Futaba Sakura (Oracle) is Claude's cousin, a prodigy hacker who was a shut‑in until the Thieves changed her heart (The Hermit, Persona Necronomicon / Prometheus). She and Claude speak in Metal Gear Solid quotes and never talk about their missing mothers directly. When Fuuka asked Claude how he and Futaba communicate, Claude said "We don't have to." Fuuka nodded like that made perfect sense.

Morgana (Mona) is a cat‑like creature created by Igor to guide the Trickster (The Magician, Persona Zorro / Mercurius). He hates Claude's toothpick habit but still naps on his lap because "you're the only one who sits still." Koromaru and Morgana have never met, but if they did, Koromaru would growl once, Morgana would hiss once, and then they would both pretend the other didn't exist.

Ryuji Sakamoto (Skull) is a loud, loyal former track star with a reputation as a delinquent (The Chariot, Persona Captain Kidd / Seiten Taisei). He calls Claude "Sensei" unironically and trains with him weekly. When Akihiko met Ryuji, they arm‑wrestled for forty‑five minutes, declared it a draw, and now send each other workout selfies at 5 AM. Claude refuses to explain this to the rest of S.E.E.S.

Ann Takamaki (Panther) is a part‑time model with a fiery heart (The Lovers, Persona Carmen / Hecate). She thinks Claude's messy hair looks like panther ears and has taken dozens of photos without his knowledge. Yukari once saw one of these photos on Ann's phone and said "That's exactly how I see him too." They now have a shared album titled "Claude Being Claude."

Yusuke Kitagawa (Fox) is an eccentric art student who spent all his money on paint (The Emperor, Persona Goemon / Kamu Susano‑o). He has attempted to paint Claude's hair seventeen times and failed, citing "the cowlicks defy perspective." When Junpei asked Yusuke why he kept trying, Yusuke said "Because his face is the most interesting shape I've ever seen." Junpei immediately understood.

Makoto Niijima (Queen) is the student council president turned strategist (The Priestess, Persona Johanna / Anat). She and Mitsuru have a mutual respect that borders on intimidation. They exchanged notes on systemic corruption once, and now Makoto sends Mitsuru monthly reports on Tokyo's political landscape. Mitsuru reads them with coffee and a small, private smile.

Haru Okumura (Noir) is a gentle heiress who gardens, brews strong coffee, and laughs terrifyingly in battle (The Empress, Persona Milady / Astarte). She and Shinjiro exchange cooking tips – she taught him how to grow basil, he taught her how to make protein‑rich stew. When Shinjiro asked why she was so kind to him, she said "Because you look like you haven't been." Shinjiro didn't speak for three days.

Sumire Yoshizawa (Violet) is a gymnast and Ren's girlfriend, a polite, hardworking first‑year (The Faith, Persona Cendrillon / Ella). She calls Claude "Claude‑san" very formally, and he calls her "Sumire" without honorifics just to watch her fluster. When Kotone asked Sumire why she tried so hard to impress Claude, Sumire said "He's Ren's brother, so he's already family." Kotone hugged her immediately.

Goro Akechi (Crow) is a detective and rival to Ren, the illegitimate son of Masayoshi Shido (The Justice, Persona Robin Hood / Loki / Hereward). He and Claude both hunt Shido for answers about their mothers, and they recognize each other's sleepless eyes. When Akihiko asked Claude if he trusted Akechi, Claude said "No." When Akihiko asked if Akechi would still show up to fight, Claude said "Yes." Akihiko accepted that as a full analysis.

Sojiro Sakura is the owner of Café Leblanc and guardian of Ren, Futaba, and Claude – a distant cousin of Tatsuya Suou. He calls Claude "the quiet one" as a compliment. When Mitsuru visited Leblanc once to discuss Claude's transfer, Sojiro made her coffee without asking. She later told Akihiko "That man understands silence better than anyone I've met."
